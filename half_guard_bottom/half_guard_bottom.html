Half Guard Bottom

Basics

Position

  • Outside foot over inside foot
  • Back heeling turning in
  • Pressure is belly down and towards us

Priorities

  • Never let them control inside space
  • Do this by controlling far shoulder, far elbow, far wrist and far knee
  • If at any point we lose this, reset and reframe

High knee shield when opponent insists on smashing

  • Frame
  • Slide knee out and up first
  • Elbow to knee contact
  • Frame away to make space

Collar tie from high knee shield

  • Transfer without loosing knee-elbow connection
  • Pressure down using knee and bodyweight, not arm

Reset their knee

  • Full body move, not arm

Off balances

  1. Knee/shin drive with optional knee tap
  2. Knee lever by pinching knees, turning right hard and bridging up slightly
  3. Pushing them back by elbow posting and sitting forward, remembering knee-elbow
  4. Collar tie pull with kick elevator
  • Alternate between these constantly
  • An opponent catching their balance is subject to attacks
  • An (good) opponent in balance will counter you every time

Flattened

  • Push them towards your feet, knee tap, bridge hard
  • Swim top frame back
  • Use high knee shield reset
  • NOTE: Make sure your head is free to move in the direction of bridge or risk rib strain
  • First direction did not work and/or they are driving very hard to the opposite side
  • Elbow clamp and knee lever
  • Follow through
